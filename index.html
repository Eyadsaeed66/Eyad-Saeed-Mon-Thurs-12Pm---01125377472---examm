Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about buying, selling, and investing in real estate

You're ready to buy a home when you have stable income, a good credit score, and a down payment saved.

Spring and early summer (March through June) are traditionally the best times to sell, as buyers are more active and homes show better with good weather. However, market conditions, local factors, and your personal timeline are more important than the season. We can help you determine the optimal timing based on current market conditions in your area.

Your offer should be based on comparable sales in the area, the home's condition, current market conditions, and your budget. In competitive markets, you might need to offer at or above asking price. Our agents will provide a detailed comparative market analysis (CMA) to help you make a competitive yet reasonable offer.

Closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the home's purchase price and include fees for loan origination, appraisal, title insurance, attorney fees, and taxes. Both buyers and sellers have closing costs, though they vary by location and can be negotiated. We'll provide a detailed breakdown of expected costs early in the process.

Yes, absolutely! A professional home inspection typically costs $300-600 but can save you thousands by identifying potential issues before you buy. We recommend inspections for all property types and can connect you with qualified, licensed inspectors in your area.

For buyers, the process typically takes 30-45 days from offer acceptance to closing, though finding the right home can take weeks or months. For sellers, the average time on market varies by location and price range, but our properties typically sell within 28 days. We'll provide realistic timelines based on your specific situation and local market conditions.
